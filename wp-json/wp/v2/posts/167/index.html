2 Kinds of Leaders<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
  • Reward centered<\/em><\/strong>
    1. They see the moving to leadership as a reward of what they\u2019ve achieved so far and is a prize.<\/li>
    2. Focus on the “glory”, “prestige”, “rewards” for achieving pinnacle of success<\/li>
    3. They usually pick and choose what they want to do as leader and do what is usually something they feel they are good at or have fun.<\/li><\/ol><\/li>
    4. Responsibility centered<\/em><\/strong>
      1. Look at this as a responsibility rather than reward.<\/li>
      2. Don\u2019t shy away from doing what is required for someone at this job<\/li>
      3. The experience of being ‘responsibility centered<\/em>‘ leader should be tough and challenging<\/li>
      4. Look at this from the perspective of the people they lead, rather than themselves.<\/li><\/ol><\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n
        • There’s no pure “Reward” \/ “Responsibility” focused Leader. People are usually in a spectrum. You need to ensure you are more aligned toward Responsibility centered.<\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n
          • Responsibility centered Leaders need to be aware of the Trap<\/strong> – when they are being responsibility centered leader, people tend to praise them and agree with them quite a lot and this can lead people to move down the path of Reward centered leadership.
            • Have a strong inner circle who can challenge and keep them honest<\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n

              5 Core responsibilities<\/strong> <\/h2>\n\n\n\n

              These are responsibilities that “Reward centered<\/em>” Leaders usually ignore or don’t do well<\/p>\n\n\n\n

              • Having difficult & uncomfortable conversations<\/em><\/strong>
                • Addressing Bad behavior immediately and not tolerating regardless of how good the direct report is at their job<\/li>
                • Don’t avoid these hard conversations<\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n
                  • Managing subordinates<\/em><\/strong>
                    • Individual coaching and guidance is necessary at all levels. This has got nothing to do with the person’s experience.<\/li>
                    • If you feel you should be able to trust people to manage themselves, or justify not knowing what your direct reports are doing by justifying not being a micromanager, then your motive for leading is wrong.<\/em><\/span><\/li>
                    • Micromanagement<\/em><\/strong> — the only people who call management, micromanagement are the ones trying to avoid accountability<\/span>.<\/li>
                    • As a leader, you need to<\/strong>
                      • Know what they are working on<\/li>
                      • Know their progress<\/li>
                      • Give them the right feedback and coaching<\/li><\/ul><\/li>
                      • Lots of experience doesn’t mean people don’t have to be managed. It’s not sign of punishment or lack of trust. It’s giving them the benefit of direction and coaching.<\/li>
                      • Best of sportsmen pay people to coach them.<\/li>
                      • Stopping managing is a sign of neglect.<\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n
                        • Running great team meetings<\/em><\/strong>
                          • Running boring meetings \/ avoiding them<\/li>
                          • Outcome based meetings<\/li>
                          • If you allow in-effective meetings to happens, this will percolate to entire org<\/li>
                          • Bad Meetings == Bad decisions<\/em><\/strong><\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n
                            • Developing Leadership team<\/em><\/strong>
                              • If you feel this is something touchy-feely and waste of time, then your motive for being a leader isn’t right.<\/li>
                              • Hiring != delegating to HR<\/li>
                              • It’s not just a annual meeting or gala for team building<\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n
                                • Communicating constantly & repeatedly<\/em><\/strong>
                                  • Research says it takes 7 times<\/strong> for people to hear the same message before believe execs are serious about it.<\/li>
                                  • CEOs need to be Chief Reminding Officers<\/strong><\/li>
                                  • You always need to over communicate.<\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n
